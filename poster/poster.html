Optimizing Traffic Light Control Using Fuzzy Logic and Particle Swarm Optimization
Saahil Mahato
Keele University
Abstract

This research introduces an intelligent traffic light control system integrating Fuzzy Logic Control (FLC) with Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) to dynamically adjust traffic light durations based on real-time traffic density. This optimizes vehicle wait times and throughput, achieving a 11.9% reduction in average system time and a 14.8% increase in throughput compared to fixed-time systems.

Introduction
Aim and Objectives

Aim: To develop and evaluate an intelligent traffic light control system that optimizes traffic flow in urban environments.

  • Develop a Fuzzy Logic Controller (FLC) for traffic light management.
  • Utilize Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) to fine-tune FLC parameters.
  • Evaluate the system's performance against traditional fixed-time control.
Rationale and Background

Urban traffic congestion is a significant problem causing delays, economic losses, and environmental impact. Traditional fixed-time traffic lights are inefficient in handling fluctuating traffic patterns.

Fuzzy Logic offers a way to model the imprecise nature of traffic flow, while PSO provides a robust method for optimizing complex systems. Integrating these techniques offers a promising approach to creating adaptive and efficient traffic management systems. This research builds upon existing work in intelligent transportation systems and aims to demonstrate the practical benefits of this combined approach.

Methodology and Approach
Fuzzy Logic Controller (FLC) Design
  • Dual-intersection control based on real-time traffic density.
  • Inputs: Traffic density at Intersections A and B (0-100).
  • Output: Light duration (10â€“60 seconds).
  • Mamdani inference system with three membership functions per variable (Low, Medium, High).
Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO) for Parameter Tuning
  • PSO optimized FLC membership function parameters.
  • Swarm Size: 20, Iterations: 50, Dimensions: 6.
  • Fitness function minimized average system time and maximized throughput.
Research Outcome and Discussion

The PSO-optimized FLC demonstrated significant improvements over the Fixed Time Controller (FTC).

Key Outcomes
  • Increased Throughput: Average throughput improved by 14.8%.
  • Reduced System Time: Average system time decreased by 11.9%.
  • More Vehicles Served: 16.0% more vehicles served overall.
Discussion

The results indicate that dynamically adjusting traffic light timings using FLC optimized by PSO leads to a more efficient traffic flow. While individual wait times at specific intersections might fluctuate, the overall system performance is significantly enhanced. The PSO effectively fine-tuned the FLC parameters to better respond to varying traffic conditions.

Conclusions

This research demonstrates that integrating PSO with Fuzzy Logic Control significantly optimizes traffic flow, outperforming traditional fixed-time systems by achieving a 11.9% reduction in average system time and a 14.8% increase in average throughput. This intelligent approach offers a promising solution for mitigating urban traffic congestion.
